Why Do We Have Mountains, Volcanoes, and Earthquakes?

Geologist in the Field / kqedquest / CC BY-NC 2.0

Have you ever wondered why Illinois is so flat when California has mountains, or why California has so many more earthquakes than the rest of the United States?

Geologists study questions like these. When they want to learn about a particular area, they go there and use clues from the area like, "Is it flat or is it mountainous? Are there earthquakes? Are there volcanoes?", to piece together the geologic history and the forces, or geologic processes, that were responsible for creating that landscape.


In this project, you will critically examine clues about the geologic history of the West Coast of North America and on the opposite coast. As you look at the geology of the area, think about why the land looks the way it does.


You will then:

  • Compare what you learn about the geological landscape of the West Coast to the geology of the East Coast.
  • Describe how the ideas of plate tectonics help explain, in part, the features that you observed.
  • Create a model that explains how the processes below the Earth's surface are related to what is observed on the Earth's surface.
